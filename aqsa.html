Al-Aqsa University

Al-Aqsa University started in the year 1955 AD as an institute for teachers under the management of the Egyptian government, and the goal at that time was to prepare and qualify teachers. In 1991, the institute developed into a college known as the College of Government Education, and since then the college has been slowly growing in its educational plans, scientific departments, professors, and students, and graduated from teachers and researchers with high scientific and educational competence from the Bachelor, Bachelor and Bachelor’s degrees through the studies program The joint higher education with Ain Shams University and the beginning of the academic year 2000/2001 AD The college turned to Al-Aqsa University.

By presidential decree on September 21, 2001 AD, Al-Aqsa University was accredited as a governmental Palestinian higher education institution in the governorates of Gaza in Palestine, as a development of the Governmental College of Education established in 1991, as a natural extension of the Institute of Teachers and Teachers, which was founded in the year 1955 AD. The university is an independent institution scientifically and academically, according to both the Higher Education Law No. 11 of 1998 and the regulations issued pursuant to it by the Ministry of Education and Higher Education. The university aims to spread knowledge, deepen its roots, and serve and develop the Palestinian society in particular, and the Arab and humanitarian community in general, within the framework of a philosophy based on national concepts and the heritage of Arab and Islamic civilization, and seeks to achieve this goal.

Al-Aqsa University is a Palestinian governmental higher education institution that aims to prepare a person who is equipped with knowledge, skills, and values, and who has the ability to continuously learn and employ information technology through capacity building programs, university education, scientific research, and community development and service. During its fulfillment of its vision, Al-Aqsa University is committed to Arab and Islamic culture and human rights principles that include responsibility, adherence to the rule of law, transparency, respect, tolerance, justice, equality, empowerment, and stakeholder participation.


Al-Aqsa admission requirements

1-The high school average must be 65% or more. 2-Student acceptance is subject to the coordination key.
3-Completion hours should be 24 hours or more.
4-The student's cumulative average is 70% or more.
5-That the student is not expelled from the university or college he was transferred from due to a disciplinary penalty.
6-The student must not have been out of school for a period of more than four semesters.
7-The student meets all the conditions for admission to Al-Aqsa University.
8-Student acceptance is subject to the coordination key.
