Bologna University

It is a public university in Bologna and the oldest university in Europe, founded in 1088. The University of Bologna is also the second largest university in Italy after the University of La Sapienza in Rome. Although the College of Constantinople in the Byzantine Empire is considered the oldest institution of higher education in history, the University of Bologna is considered, according to historians, the oldest university in the modern concept of higher education. The university began as a cathedral school or monastic school linked to the Catholic Church, and then quickly separated as the number of students increased.

In 23 colleges there are about 100,000 registered students. Since 1989, the University has been operating in addition to its headquarters in Bologna, in other departments also in Cesena, Forli, Ravenna, and Rimini. In 1998 a branch was opened in Buenos Aires.


Bologna admission requirements

You can apply for Unibo Action 1&2 if:

-you are in possession of (or about to obtain) a valid qualification for access to your chosen degree programme, issued by an Institution outside of the Italian education system. Students holding a diploma issued by an Italian school established outside Italy can apply as well;
-you will take one of the following tests by the application deadline:
  * if you are interested in registering in a First or Single Cycle Degree Programme, the SAT test;
  * if you are interested in registering in a Second Cycle Degree Programme, the GRE test.
-you are less than 30 years old upon the deadline of the call for application.

The SAT and GRE are aptitude and skills assessment tests which can be taken in authorised centres in several countries around the world. The tests are in English. You must register for the tests on the websites of managing institutions.

You can find more information in the call for applications.
