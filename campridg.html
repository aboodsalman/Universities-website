Cambridge University

The University of Cambridge is a public research university in Cambridge, UK. The University of Cambridge was founded in 1209 and King Henry III
granted the Royal Charter in 1231, and is the second oldest English-speaking university and the second largest university in the world still alive

The University has taught many prominent graduates including prominent mathematicians, scholars, politicians, lawyers, philosophers, writers, actors and
heads of foreign countries, and as of October 2018 there were 118 Nobel Prize winners, 11 medals, 6 Turing Award winners and 15 prime ministers
The British are affiliated with Cambridge as students, alumni, faculty or research teams.
