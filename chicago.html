Chicago University

It is a private university located in the suburb of Hyde Park and was established in 1890. The first president and member of the university faculty was Professor William Rene Harper. The university holds the top ten position in several US and international arrangements for universities.

The academic year of the university is run on a quarterly basis and is divided into four semesters: Summer (June - August), Fall (September - December), Winter (January - March), and Spring (April - June). Full-time undergraduate students must take three to four courses approximately every three months before their quarterly academic breaks. The school year usually begins in late September and ends in mid-June. The sports team colors are maroon and white. Known as a maroon, the mascot is called Phil Phoenix.



Chicago admission requirements

The rate of student acceptance to study at the University of Chicago is 8%, the lowest in college history and the lowest in the country.

In 2018, the University of Chicago became the first major research university to no longer need SAT / ACT degrees from college applicants. Tuition and fees at University of Chicago are estimated at 2019-2020 at 57,263. The average GPA at the University of Chicago is 4.29. The average score for a SAT score at University of Chicago is 1540 on a SAT 1600 scale.

On the old 2400 SAT, this corresponds to an average SAT score of 2260. The average ACT degree at the University of Chicago is 34. This result makes the University of Chicago very competitive for ACT degrees.

General, electronic and global applications are available and accepted although additional forms are required. An essay or personal statement must be submitted to all new students with a minimum of 2 letters of recommendation. A $ 75 fee is required and fee exemption is available.

Test requirements include; results for SAT, ACT, SAT, ACT (optional), or SAT tests in Office prior to February 3. Subjects required include English, mathematics, science, foreign languages, social studies, history, and options.
