Cornell University

It is an American private university founded in 1865 in Ithaca, New York. Cornell University is considered one of the oldest and richest American universities, despite being the latest member of the American University's Ivy League. This association includes the best and most prestigious American universities that have won Nobel Prizes, and 43 people, who were and still have a link to the university, won Nobel Prizes in all fields.

Cornell University is often ranked among the top ten universities in the Americas, and it is ranked among the top twenty universities in the world in various international rankings.


Cornell admission requirements

There are four conditions for admission to Cornell University:

First: The need for a student applying to study at the university to obtain a high school diploma or (H.S.D certificate).

Second: The student must pass the TOFEL or IELTS test, as it is one of the most prominent tests for measuring and mastering the English language level, but they are distinct tests that cannot be passed easily except after careful study of the language, and therefore some American universities give students in other universities some scholarships Educational program to pass and complete one of these exams.

Third: The student must have proof of financial ability, as the nature of study in American universities needs to pay an annual amount of money, so the student must prove his ability to pay the costs of the study by providing a document proving that he has a bank account.

Fourth: The university requires the availability of some documents to complete postgraduate studies, as it requires a curriculum vitae and research plan for the subject of study, in addition to providing the student with a certificate of experience in the field in which he would like to complete postgraduate studies and a letter of recommendation from one of the teachers and professors he has learned before.
