Massachusetts Institute of Technology

It is a university in Cambridge, Massachusetts, founded in 1861. This institute is a world-class institute. The institute has employed many great scholars such as Norbert Wiener; its primary mission is education and research in the practical applications of science and technology, and the institute is divided into five schools and one college that contains 34 academic disciplines and 53 laboratories. The institute played a major role in biomedical engineering, in the development of computers, and in the navigation devices used in missiles and spacecraft. It has 10,000 students of multiple nationalities. The Institute's 960 faculty are known for excellence and excellence in the field of advanced technical research and its applications, with 85 of them being awarded the Nobel Prize.


Massachusetts admission requirements

First: training courses and scientific skill

If you want to enroll in college at the institute, you must be familiar with some skills to enhance your chances of being accepted, namely:

-You have studied English for at least four years.
-Being familiar with the rules and laws of mathematics up to at least the level of differential and integral studies, especially when wanting to enroll in engineering school.
-You have also studied and are familiar with the most important laws, theories, and rules of chemistry, biology, and physics.
-If you want to go to theoretical schools, you should study history and social studies for at least two years.

Second: Pass some tests

For international or non-English speaking students, they must pass the following tests:

Successfully pass the SAT test, or pass the ACT test with only two SAT subjects (mathematics with chemistry, biology, or physics).
Alternatively, the TOEFL can be taken in the English language with two SAT exams, one in mathematics and one in science.
