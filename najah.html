An-Najah University

An-Najah National University is a Palestinian non-governmental public university governed by a board of Trustees. It is located in Nablus, in the nortern West Bank. The university has 22,000 students and 300 professors in 19 faculties. It is the largest Palestinian university.

Most of the students are Palestinian, but there are also students and professors from all over the world. Languages spoken on campus include Arabic, Hebrew, English, French, and Spanish.

The university president is Professor Dr. Rami Hamdallah. The Vice-President for Academic Affairs is Professor Dr. Maher Natsheh and the Vice-President for Administration Affairs is Dr. Shaker AlBitar.
Ansam Sawalha, who is the Dean of the Faculty of Pharmacy, is the first Palestinian woman named to the Women in Science Hall of Fame in 2011. Sawalha was honored for her achievement of establishing the first Poison Control and Drug Information Center in the Palestinian Territories in 2006.
The university has sixteen scientific faculties and humanities faculties. An-Najah offers undergraduate instruction in the fields of medicine, engineering, humanities, social sciences and the natural sciences, as well as courses of graduate study in humanities and the social sciences.


An-Najah admission requirements

The type of General Secondary Education Certificate and the student’s cumulative average determine his/her admission to the various faculties at the university. Students of the literary stream may study at the Faculties of Humanities; students who hold scientific stream certificates are accepted into the programs offered by all faculties, and those who hold a vocational stream certificate are accepted into the Faculty of Information Technology, the Faculty of Engineering, the Faculty of Fine Arts, the Faculty of Physical Education, and the Technology Education Department at the Education Sciences Faculty. As for the holders of the commercial stream certificate, they can apply for the programs offered by the Faculty of Economics and Administrative Sciences, the Faculty of Sports, and the Faculty of Fine Arts.

In order to be accepted into the Faculty of Fine Arts, the Faculty of Physical Education, and the Department of Architectural Engineering, students have to pass a placement test. All students who are admitted to the University have to take the English Placement Test and the Computer Placement Test. Those who fail the placement tests must take courses 32100 and 10100 respectively.

Entry Qualifications

1-A minimum average of 65% is required for admission to the Faculty of Agriculture, the Faculty of Sciences and the Faculty of Humanities.
2-A minimum Average of 75% is required for admission to the Faculty of Law and the Faculty of Information Technology.
3-A minimum Average of 75% is required for admission to the Faculty of Veterinary Medicine and the Faculty of Nursing.
4-A minimum average of 80% is required for admission to the Faculty of Engineering and the Faculty of Optometry.
5-A minimum average of 85% is required for admission to the Faculty of Pharmacy.

6-A minimum average of 90% is required for admission to the Faculty of Human Medicine. Students are admitted competitively to the Bachelor's Degree program based on their cumulative average in the General Secondary Education Certificate (Tawjihi), which must be above the minimum average specified by the Ministry of Education. The number of students to be admitted to the University at the beginning of each academic year is decided by the Council of Deans and approved by the Council of Trustees, taking into consideration the market demands in Palestine and the neighboring Arab countries, and the importance of elevating the cultural, technical, and professional level of the country.
