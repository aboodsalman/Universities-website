Toreno University

It is an Italian university based in Turin. It is one of the oldest universities in Europe that still performs its research and educational role.

The University of Turin is a reputable educational center in the region of Piedmont and one of Italy's oldest universities, and the university has produced a momentum of eminent personalities, among them a number of prime ministers of Italy, three Nobel Prize winners, lawyers, philosophers and writers


Toreno admission requirements

-The scholarship is available to all students of different nationalities of the world except Italian citizens.

-The applicant must not be a holder of or reside in Italian citizenship.

-It is available to international students with university degrees from any country in the world and Italian university degrees are not accepted.

-The student admitted to the scholarship needs the Italian language in his academic and social life during the period of study at the university, so the scholarship program at the University of Turin provides a preparatory course for the Italian language for all students admitted who do not master the Italian language before the start of the scholarship program.

-The best applications are submitted based on the preference of GRE certificates. Applications that do not contain this certificate are not accepted and must be obtained no later than 5 years before the deadline for the grant.

-This exam must be successfully passed in the three departments with the following minimum scores:
*Verbal reasoning: 140/170
*Quantitative thinking: 140/170
*Analytical Writing: 3.5 / 6.
